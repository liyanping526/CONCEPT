Williansburg

Williamsburg, Brooklyn is an eclectic neighborhood, nestled between Bushwick and Greenpoint. It’s population is a melting pot made up primarily of Puerto Ricans, Dominicans, Hasidic Jews, Poles, Italians, and the blanket term for all of the newbie’s.. “Hipsters”. The original Williamsburg residents were immigrants. They were prompted to move there due to poor living conditions in Lower East Side tenements, forcing them across the East River. Later, drawn to the area by low rents and large floor area, an artist community began to emerge between the 1970-90’s. Although, in large, these creatives were priced out of the area as a result of gentrification, their roots in WB have shaped it into what it has become today. Despite all of the hype and controversy that has surrounded Williamsburg in recent years, the residents and frequenters can vouch that there is always something new to do or a homey place to hang your hat. From $4 beers (Rosemary’s) to $100 steaks (Peter Lugers), skylines and sunsets (Greenpoint-Williamsburg Waterfront) to rooftop ragers (Output), boutiques (Le Grand Strip) to oddity shops (Mother of Junk) there is something for everyone on any budget.

By Christina McWilliams
